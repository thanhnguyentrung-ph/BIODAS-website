Study in the UK for young global leaders

APPLY NOW


The BIODAS Team is pleased to share information about the Chevening Scholarship ‚Äì the UK government's prestigious scholarship program for outstanding individuals with a desire to create positive change in their communities, countries, and globally. This is an opportunity to study for a one-year master's degree in the UK completely free of charge, opening up a journey of academic and professional development and a sustainable international leadership network.

Official website: https://www.chevening.org

1. Introduction to the Chevening Scholarship

Chevening Scholarships enable promising young leaders from around the world to pursue a one-year master's degree at leading universities in the UK. If you have passion, influence, and a desire to solve local or global challenges, Chevening is the opportunity for you.

  • Professional and academic development.
  • Expand your international network.
  • Experience British culture.
  • Build a long-lasting cooperative relationship with the UK.

2. Who can apply for the Chevening Scholarship?

Chevening does not have any restrictions on age, gender, religion, marital status, or profession. What matters is your leadership mindset, clear goals, and potential to make a positive impact.

‚úÖ Eligibility requirements:

  • Be a citizen of an eligible country.
  • Commit to returning to your home country for at least 2 years after the scholarship ends.
  • Have at least 2,800 hours of work experience.
  • Have a bachelor's degree that qualifies you for a master's degree in the UK.
  • Apply for 3 different courses and have at least 1 unconditional offer of admission.

‚ùå Not eligible if:

  • Have British or dual British citizenship.
  • Have previously studied in the UK with another UK government scholarship.
  • Are an employee or relative of a UK government agency in the last 2 years.

3. Work experience

Applicants need at least 2,800 hours of work experience (equivalent to 2 years full-time), which can be calculated from:

  • Full-time or part-time employment.
  • Volunteer activities.
  • Paid or unpaid internships.

4. Chevening Application Guide

  1. Check your eligibility.
  2. Choose 3 master's courses in the UK.
  3. Prepare your application and letters of recommendation.
  4. Apply online through the Chevening system.
  5. Attend an interview and submit an unconditional offer of admission.

5. Preparing for the Chevening interview

Prepare thoroughly: master the content of your application, your career orientation, and demonstrate your leadership ability and confident English communication skills.

6. Choosing a course in the UK

Applicants need to choose 3 eligible master's programs from universities in the UK.
Find a suitable course (Chevening Course Finder)

7. Improving English skills

Although Chevening does not require an English language certificate when applying, most UK universities do. Hone your skills to write better essays and perform better in interviews.
