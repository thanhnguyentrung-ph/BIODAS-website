JICA “SDGS GLOBAL LEADER 2026” FULLY FUNDED SCHOLARSHIP

Opportunity to pursue a Master's/PhD in Japan for officials of the Vietnamese Government

APPLY NOW


The BIODAS Team is pleased to share information about the JICA “SDGs Global Leader 2026” Scholarship – a fully funded scholarship program of the Government of Japan through the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) for young and mid-career government officials, as well as potential lecturers/researchers. This is an opportunity to pursue a master's (2 years) or doctoral (3 years) degree at leading Japanese universities completely free of charge, opening up a journey of policy capacity development and a sustainable Vietnam-Japan cooperation network.

Official website: https://www.jica.go.jp/vietnam/index.html

Contact JICA Vietnam:

  • Ms. Nguyen Thi Mai Khanh
  • Phone: 84-4-38315005~8 (Ext. 142)
  • Email: nguyenthimaikhanh.vt@jica.go.jp

Source: Official Letter No. 722.25/JICA-GI dated September 3, 2025 of the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA) Office in Vietnam

1. Introduction to the JICA “SDGs Global Leader” Scholarship

The “SDGs Global Leader” program is part of the JICA Knowledge Co-Creation Program under the Official Development Assistance (ODA) framework of the Government of Japan. The program aims to develop high-quality human resources capable of contributing to the formulation and implementation of sustainable development policies. Study duration: 2 years (Master's) or 3 years (Doctoral).

  • Policy capacity development: Learn from Japan's unique development experience.
  • Expand international network: Connect with future leaders from 111 countries.
  • Full scholarship: Covers tuition, living expenses, airfare, and insurance.
  • Special JICA-DSP program: Participate in activities, internships at Japanese public/private organizations.

2. Who can apply for the JICA Scholarship?

The scholarship is for young/mid-career government officials, lecturers, or researchers who can contribute to policy and social development in Vietnam.

✅ Eligibility requirements:

  • Vietnamese citizen eligible for Japanese ODA.
  • Currently working in a government agency, research institute, or university.
  • Age: ≤35 (Master's), ≤40 (Doctoral) as of April 1, 2026.
  • Degree: Bachelor's (for Master's) or Master's (for Doctoral).
  • English: TOEFL iBT ≥80 or IELTS ≥6.5.
  • Must be physically present in Japan throughout the course.

❌ Not eligible if:

  • Currently receiving or will receive another scholarship.
  • Belong to the military or related organizations.
  • Cannot be physically present in Japan.

3. Priority research fields

  • Public Administration/Public Policy
  • Finance/Public Financial Management
  • International Politics/Diplomacy
  • Regional Studies
  • Business/International Trade/Investment
  • Disaster Risk Reduction
  • Maritime Issues

4. JICA Application Guide

Applicants must be nominated by their home institution and approved by the Government of Vietnam. Applications must be submitted through the Ministry of Finance of Vietnam no later than October 22, 2025.

Application process:

  1. Nomination at the home institution (early September 2025)
  2. Selection by the higher-level agency (early November 2025)
  3. Submission of application to JICA through the Ministry of Finance (October 22, 2025)
  4. Screening by JICA (November 2025)
  5. Pre-matching with the university (December 2025)
  6. Official entrance examination (February–June 2026)
  7. Examination results (June–July 2026)
  8. Preparation for departure (June–August 2026)
  9. Enrollment (August–October 2026)

Required documents:

  • JICA Application Form (including: personal information, educational background, nominating institution, work experience, statement of commitment)
  • Graduation certificate (notarized copy, in English or with an official translation)
  • Academic transcript (must contain all grades of all subjects, notarized copy, in English or with a translation)
  • English language certificate (TOEFL iBT ≥80 or IELTS ≥6.5)
  • Passport or ID card with photo (to verify nationality, name, gender, date of birth)
  • 2 photos 3x4cm (pasted on the application form)
  • Health certificate (examination date must be after April 1, 2026 and submitted to JICA before April 30, 2026)

5. Preparing for the selection process

JICA evaluates applications based on the research plan and potential for policy contribution. Partner universities will pre-match, after which candidates will participate in the official entrance examination or interview.

6. Choosing a university and program of study

Applicants choose a university from JICA's partner list (provided by JICA Vietnam). Each university may have its own requirements.

7. Improving English skills

The program is taught in English. The cost of the English test may be reimbursed if taken within the same Japanese fiscal year (April 1 – March 31 of the following year).

9. Costs covered by JICA

  • Tuition: Actual cost according to the university
  • Living expenses: 117,000–148,000 yen/month
  • Airfare: Actual cost (round trip)
  • Outfit allowance: 100,000 yen
  • Moving allowance: 164,000–224,000 yen
  • Research expenses: Up to 360,000 yen/year
  • Health insurance: According to regulations

10. Participation conditions

Selected applicants must comply with JICA's conditions, including being present in Japan, not changing flight tickets, not extending the course, not plagiarizing, not using generative AI in their application or research, and completing the course on time.

11. Why choose the JICA Scholarship?

  • Full scholarship covering all expenses.
  • Study at prestigious Japanese universities.
  • Special JICA-DSP program and practical internship.
  • Global network with 111 partner countries.
  • Sustainable and professional Vietnam-Japan bridge.
