  1. Overview

In the context of the 4.0 Industrial Revolution, data is considered the “new oil” of the knowledge economy. Effective data mining has become a core competency in healthcare, education, economics, public administration, and community development. However, many organizations and communities in Vietnam still face significant challenges. 

 <p><strong>Key challenges:</strong></p>
 <ul>
   <li>Lack of systematic, practical guidance documents in Vietnamese.</li>
   <li>Uneven data analysis capacity, still dependent on manual tools (basic Excel, paper reports).</li>
   <li>Lack of connection between modern analysis tools (R, Python, Power BI, Stata) and practical applications in community management, public health, and sustainable development.</li>
 </ul>
 <p><strong>Recent international studies show:</strong></p>
 <ul>
   <li><em>Al Nuaimi &amp; Awofeso (2024):</em> Data and Business Intelligence (BI) are drivers for improving the efficiency of the healthcare system.</li>
   <li><em>Dicuonzo et al. (2022):</em> BI plays a central role in the digital transformation of healthcare.</li>
   <li><em>Mansoor &amp; Dar (2024):</em> The application of BI helps optimize the management of clinical testing processes.</li>
 </ul>

Therefore, it is necessary to develop an academic and practical handbook to guide data processing and analysis for the community, combining theory, tools, methods, and practical illustrations.

  1. Objectives

2.1. General Objective

To compile a practical handbook that is scientific, academic, but easy to access, to enhance the data processing and analysis capacity for communities, social organizations, and management agencies in Vietnam in the digital age.

2.2. Specific Objectives

  1. Present a systematic theory of data, data quality, and data governance in the community.
  2. Guide the use of popular tools: Excel / Power Query / Power Pivot, Power BI, Stata, R / RMarkdown / RShiny, basic Python, Quarto / Positron.
  3. Introduce analysis methods: from descriptive statistics, inferential analysis to Big Data and Business Intelligence (BI).
  4. Illustrate through practical case studies:
    • Application of BI in test management (Navify);
    • Big Data in the health supply chain;
    • Community dashboard using Power BI;
    • Public health data analysis using RShiny. 
    </li>
<li>Provide a training framework, sample datasets, exercises, and implementation guidance.</li>

  1. Scope and Audience

Beneficiaries:

  • Healthcare workers
  • Statisticians – managers
  • Civil society organizations
  • Students – young researchers
  • Government officials

Scope of application:

  • Public health
  • Community governance
  • Education
  • Socio-economics
  • Environment

Limitations:

The handbook focuses on applied data practice, and does not replace statistics textbooks or advanced programming materials.

  1. Proposed Structure

Part I. Data Fundamentals and Data Governance

  • Concepts and classification of data (structured, unstructured, semi-structured).
  • Data lifecycle: collection – cleaning – storage – analysis – reporting – application.
  • Data quality: attributes (accuracy, completeness, timeliness, integrity).
  • FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable).
  • Data security and community research ethics.

Part II. Data Processing Tools

  • Excel & Power Query/Power Pivot.
  • Power BI for reports and dashboards.
  • Stata: analysis of social and health quantitative data.
  • R & RMarkdown & RShiny: from basic analysis to web applications.
  • Python: pandas, matplotlib, scikit-learn for basic data analysis and automation.
  • Quarto/Positron: reproducible research, publishing scientific reports.

Part III. Data Analysis Methods

  • Descriptive statistics and visualization.
  • Linear and logistic regression.
  • Multivariate analysis: PCA, EFA, Cluster Analysis.
  • Latent Class Analysis (LCA) and Bayesian LCA.
  • Big Data Analytics and Business Intelligence: from theory to application.

Part IV. Data Visualization and Reporting

  • Scientific and aesthetic principles of data visualization.
  • Tool comparison: Excel, Power BI, ggplot2 (R), matplotlib (Python).
  • Dynamic reporting with RMarkdown, Quarto.
  • BI dashboards for health and social sectors.

Part V. Practical Applications

  • Health: disease forecasting, vaccine supply chain management.
  • Test management: data standardization, reducing processing time, and providing real-time dashboards.
  • Community governance: survey analysis, budget monitoring.
  • Education and society: analysis of training quality, labor market.

Part VI. Case Studies

  • Box 1: Business Intelligence in testing.
  • Box 2: Big Data in health supply chain management (COVID-19).
  • Box 3: Power BI dashboard for community surveys.
  • Box 4: RShiny for public health data.

Part VII. Data Infrastructure and Governance

  • Data storage systems (cloud, open data, big data warehouse).
  • Data integration from multiple sources.
  • International standards: HL7, FHIR (health), SDMX (social).

Part VIII. Training and Scaling

  • Design short-term training programs (3–5 days).
  • Develop an open resource repository: datasets, scripts, video tutorials.
  • Build a data practice community.

  1. Development Methodology

Implementation Process

  1. Systematic literature review: referencing recent studies on Big Data, Business Intelligence (BI), and community data management (e.g., Dicuonzo, 2022; Al Nuaimi, 2024).
  2. Expert interviews & workshops: working with statisticians, BI experts, public health officials to gather insights and practical requirements.
  3. Pilot implementation: applying the materials at hospitals, universities, and community organizations to evaluate effectiveness and applicability.
  4. Editing & standardization: combining academic foundations with practical illustrations; standardizing terminology, processes, and lesson structures.
  5. Feedback loop: gathering feedback from pilot users, improving content and accompanying tools.

Open Access Publishing:

  • Release interactive PDF and website.
  • Publicly release project source code (scripts, templates) on GitHub.
  • Develop an online course (MOOC) with sample datasets and exercises.

  1. Expected Outcomes

Deliverables

  • An academic–practical handbook (≈ 200–250 pages).
  • A set of illustrative case studies with dashboards, source code, and sample data.
  • An open online resource repository (documents, scripts, templates).

Training Implementation

  • Organize at least 03 pilot training courses.
  • Reach a minimum of 200 trainees (from various fields).

Expected impact: Contribute to enhancing digital capacity for communities and social organizations, promoting data-driven decision-making practices.
